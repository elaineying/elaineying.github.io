ABOUT "DISAPPEARING TRADITIONS"

As China continues to blossom through industrialization, many of its time-honored crafts and arts are disappearing.

Traditions such as flower lanterns, lime carving and the Yue Opera enjoy long histories, yet they face a great problem in finding caretakers among the next generation.

Hong Ying, a multimedia journalist, travelled to Zhejiang, China to document these cultural treasures and the struggle China's older generation faces in keeping them alive.

ABOUT HONG YING

Hong Ying is a multimedia storyteller with international reporting experience.

Her specialties include video documentary field work and production, photography, on-camera reporting, infographic design, writing and web design.

Hong previously worked as a journalist at Zhejiang Radio and TV Center in 2014, traveling across China for the program One Degree Deeper. Other experiences include designing infographics for Univision for the 2014 Midterm Election and creating original video packages for UMTV.

Prior to attending the University of Miami to pursue a Master's degree focusing on Multimedia Journalism, Hong studied Chinese Linguistics and Literature at Shanxi University in Taiyuan, China.
